Description

Tequeño is a culinary preparation originating from Venezuela that typically consists of a fried wheat flour dough, filled with Venezuelan white cheese. It is consumed as an appetizer, breakfast, fast food or snack.

Recipe Information

  • 410 g of flour without baking powder
  • 240 g cold butter, cubed
  • 1 tsp Gourmet Sea Salt
  • 1 egg
  • 120 ml of water
  • 500 g of hard white cheese such as llanero or mozzarella
  • 1 liter of oil for frying

Steps

  1. In a bowl, put the flour, butter and salt. With your fingers, pinch the butter into the rest of the ingredients until you have a texture like wet sand.
  2. Add the water and mix until a dough forms. Wrap in film and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  3. Roll out the dough on a floured surface until it is 2mm thick.
  4. Cut 3cm wide strips from the dough.
  5. Cut the llanero cheese into 2 cm strips and wrap them with the dough as if it were a mummy. Once wrapped, rotate them on the table to seal the layers.
  6. Preheat the oil over medium heat until it reaches 180°C. Add the tequeños and fry for 3-4 minutes or until golden.
